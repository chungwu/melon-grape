How They Met

Mel and Chung both went to UC Berkeley for college. They met long before they started dating.

The first time their paths crossed was on a night in September, 2004 at La Val's Subterranean Theater, a cramped, dark basement under the popular pizza parlor off of Euclid Ave that served as theater space for budding theater troupes. It was Chung's idea, really; he loved the theater, and Julie, a close friend, was cajoled into going. For fear of total boredom, Julie decided to bring her roommate, Mel, along. The play that night was Fluffy Bunnies in a Field of Daisies, though in truth, neither Mel nor Chung remember much of the play, except for the uncomfortable chairs that they had to sit in. It was difficult at first for Mel to understand and share Chung's enthusiasm of the art form due to its underwhelming production values. Indeed, it was harder still for her to appreciate Chung, the quiet, reserved guy who barely spoke all night.

Nevertheless, Mel managed to be slightly interested, by both the play and the person. She tried to strike up a conversation with Chung on AIM (back when it was cool), but then made the mistake of asking for technical support for her computer. Chung never replied back, and Mel vowed to never again speak to Julie's snobby friend.

In the fall of 2005, Mel and Chung's paths collided again when fate threw Mel and Chung's sister, Fu, together as roommates. Chung had graduated by then and was working at Google, but he still went to Berkeley frequently to visit his sister. It was then that Mel and Chung finally started getting to know each other. They went to several more plays, including Travesties, a play so confounding that Mel and Chung had an extended conversation about it into the wee hours of early morning. Mel found Chung's take on the play strikingly similar to hers; she may one day call this conversation a turning point of sorts. These discussions continued afterwards on AIM (it was still cool) on everything and anything, and they never stopped talking to each other and sharing their experiences with each other ever since.
